The CAP theorem states that a distributed system can guarantee at most two of three properties: consistency, availability, and partition tolerance. Since network partitions are unavoidable in distributed systems, the practical choice is between CP (consistency during partitions, sacrificing availability) and AP (availability during partitions, risking stale reads). HBase and Zookeeper are CP systems that refuse writes during partition. Cassandra and CouchDB are AP systems that continue accepting writes and reconcile differences later. PACELC extends CAP by adding latency as a dimension: even without partitions, there is a trade-off between latency and consistency. Most modern databases offer tunable consistency levels that let applications choose where they sit on the spectrum.
