Split-brain occurs when a distributed system partitions into two or more groups that each believe they are the sole active primary and continue accepting writes independently. When the partition heals, the system has divergent state that must be reconciled. Prevention mechanisms include requiring quorum acknowledgment before writes, fencing tokens that invalidate stale primaries, and STONITH (shoot the other node in the head) mechanisms that forcibly terminate suspected primaries before a new one is promoted. Fencing tokens are monotonically increasing integers issued by the lock service; a primary must include its current token in every write, and storage nodes reject writes with stale tokens. ZooKeeper ephemeral nodes are commonly used for leader election with implicit fencing.
