Leader election algorithms select a single coordinator from a group of processes to act as the primary for operations that require centralized coordination. The bully algorithm has the highest-ID live process assert leadership by sending election messages and suppressing lower-ID candidates. The ring algorithm passes an election token around a logical ring, accumulating process IDs until the token returns to its originator. Modern production systems use distributed lock services: ZooKeeper's ephemeral sequential nodes let processes compete for a well-known path with the lowest-sequence holder becoming leader. etcd's lease mechanism grants leadership to the process holding the lock, with automatic revocation when the lease expires and the holder fails to renew. The critical invariant is that at most one process believes it is leader at any given time, enforced through lease expiry and fencing tokens.
