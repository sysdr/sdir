Circuit breakers prevent cascading failures by stopping calls to a dependency that is failing or slow. The circuit has three states: closed (requests pass through normally), open (requests fail immediately without calling the dependency), and half-open (a probe request tests whether the dependency has recovered). The circuit opens when the error rate or response time exceeds a threshold over a rolling time window. Half-open state sends a single test request; if it succeeds, the circuit closes; if it fails, it returns to open. Netflix Hystrix popularized circuit breakers on the JVM; resilience4j is the modern replacement. At high request rates, the rolling window requires bucketed counters rather than individual event storage. The critical operational consideration is tuning the threshold to avoid nuisance trips during brief spikes while catching real failures quickly.
